Community Bulletin

The local council has announced new initiatives for sustainability, including expanded recycling programs and the development of community gardens in public parks. These projects aim to reduce waste and engage residents in greener practices.

In addition to environmental efforts, the council is launching a new series of weekend workshops on financial literacy, career development, and wellness. These workshops will be open to all residents free of charge.

Volunteers are also needed for the annual Neighborhood Clean-Up event scheduled for July. This year’s effort will focus on riverside restoration and graffiti removal throughout the downtown area.

Stay informed through the city’s official website and bulletin boards posted at community centers. Together, we can build a more vibrant, connected, and sustainable neighborhood.
